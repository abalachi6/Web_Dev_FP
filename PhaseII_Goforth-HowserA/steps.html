Steps to Composting:

You will first need to choose a compost bin. You can purchase them at your local garden store, or you can just buy a trash bin designated for your compost! Alternatively you can make your compost in heaps. However you would need lots of space and ample amounts of compostable material.
Your next step should be to choose where you will keep your composter. Yout want it to be somewhere convienent so that you will use it. Don't put it too close to any doors or windows though. While composts aren't supposed to smell strongly, if you are new to composting you might want to error on the side of caution and keep it at bay.
You will build your compost in layers of "green" material and "brown" material. When first starting your compost you will want a very course material such as sticks. This will allow the compost to drain well. From there you will alternate between green and brown materials. Your green material will be items like green leaves, uncooked veggie and fruit remnants, egg shels, and flowers. Your brown material will be items like dried leaves, paper egg cartons, bark, dryler lint, small cut cardboard, dead plants, and light papers such as coffee filters.
You will need to maintain your compost. This involves making sure your compost is moist, but not wet or dry. You should give your compost a good stir at least once a week. When you add in new green material you should mix it into the lower layers of your compost. A well maintained compost should have a very minimal odor, if any at all.
Your compost will take about 4-6 months to finish. Once finished it should be dark in color, crumbly, and smell very earthy. The finished compost will be at the top of your bin. You can scoop it out and leave the rest to finish working. Be sure it is finished before you use it as it could harm your plants if there is still some decomposing occuring.
